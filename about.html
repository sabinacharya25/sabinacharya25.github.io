About Me

Sabin Acharya is a PhD student at the University of Nevada, Reno, specializing in structural engineering, with a focus on improving the safety of rebar cages using U-bolt connectors. His research involves utilizing advanced techniques like Target-Tracking Digital Image Correlation (DIC) for full-scale rebar cage testing. He is working on developing tools to measure deformation in rebar cages and create 3D models using digital image correlation methods, a process that combines his passion for structural safety and cutting-edge technology. Sabin holds a master's degree in structural engineering and a solid background in analytical modeling and computational methods.

In addition to his research, Sabin is expanding his knowledge in Python, image processing, and computer vision techniques to further his work on DIC tools. As someone who enjoys working independently in a lab environment, Sabin is dedicated to building peace of mind, consistency, and self-determination while navigating complex engineering problems. He is passionate about continuously improving his skills and understanding in both structural engineering and computational tools.With a mindset focused on learning and growth, Sabin aims to contribute meaningful advancements in the field of structural engineering.
